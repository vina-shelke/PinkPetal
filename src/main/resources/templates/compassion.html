PMS Emotional Wellness Tips
  • Acknowledge & Accept: Don't fight your feelings. It's completely valid to feel sad, low, or irritated.
  • Say No & Delegate: Cycle fatigue is real. Drop non-urgent tasks and prioritize rest.
  • Reduce Triggers: Limit coffee and heavy sugar. They can intensify mood fluctuations and cramps.
  • Self-Soothe: Take hot showers, wrap in comfortable clothes, and sip warm tea.
Mental Health Awareness: PMDD

What is PMDD? Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD) is a severe form of PMS, affecting about 5% of women. It leads to intense depressive episodes, severe anxiety, and panic attacks starting 1-2 weeks before your period.

Practicing Self-Compassion: Talk to yourself like a best friend. Allow yourself to make mistakes. Sleep when you need to—resting is a form of active care for your hormonal health.
