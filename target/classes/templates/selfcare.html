PMS Emotional Wellness Tips

Hormonal shifts can cause emotional turbulence. Practicing these tips helps maintain emotional balance during heavy cycle phases.

  • Acknowledge & Accept: Don't fight your feelings. It's completely valid to feel sad, low, or irritated.
  • Say No & Delegate: Cycle fatigue is real. Drop non-urgent tasks and prioritize rest.
  • Reduce Triggers: Limit coffee and heavy sugar. They can intensify mood fluctuations and cramps.
  • Self-Soothe: Take hot showers, wrap in comfortable clothes, and sip warm tea.
Mental Health: Understanding PMDD

What is PMDD? Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD) is a severe, chronic medical condition affecting about 5% of women. It goes far beyond typical PMS, causing severe depressive episodes, extreme anxiety, and intense mood shifts starting 1-2 weeks before the period starts.

Self-Compassion practice: Be gentle with yourself when hormones fluctuate. Remind yourself that these intense emotional waves are driven by physiological shifts, not personal failure. Talk to yourself like a dear friend and prioritize sleep and peace.
