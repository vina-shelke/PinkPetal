Anatomy Education

Uterus Structure & Anatomy

Click on the soft hotspot dots in the interactive anatomical diagram below to explore the reproductive system organs and understand how they work.

Uterus (Womb) 🤰

A hollow, muscular, pear-shaped organ where a fetus develops during pregnancy. The inner lining, called the endometrium, thickens each month in preparation for a fertilized egg. If pregnancy does not occur, this lining sheds as your period.

Hormones 101

Understanding Your Hormones

Hormones are the chemical messengers driving your menstrual cycle. They affect far more than reproduction; they shape your skin, sleep, energy, emotions, and appetite. 🌸

Estrogen ✨

Role: Develops egg follicles and rebuilds the uterine lining.

Effects: Estrogen boosts mood, skin glow, and energy levels during the follicular phase.

Balance tip: Stay active
Progesterone 🧘‍♀️

Role: Prepares the uterus for implantation and maintains lining.

Effects: Promotes calm, sleep, and appetite, but drops trigger PMS and cramps.

Balance tip: Prioritize rest
Luteinizing (LH) ⚡

Role: Triggers the release of a mature egg from the ovary.

Effects: The LH surge acts as the final trigger for ovulation. Energy is at its peak!

Balance tip: Stay hydrated
FSH 🌀

Role: Stimulates egg follicle growth in the ovaries at start of cycle.

Effects: Gently wakes the reproductive system, starting the follicular phase energy curve.

Balance tip: Nourishing food

Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5)

What happens: Low levels of estrogen and progesterone cause the uterine lining to break down and shed, resulting in period bleeding.

  • Hormones: Low Estrogen & Progesterone
  • Mood: Introspective, quiet, nesting instinct, may feel fatigue
  • Common symptoms: Cramping, lower back ache, mild fatigue
  • Self-care tip: Keep warm, drink iron-rich teas, and choose light walking or gentle yoga. 🧘‍♀️
Follicular Phase (Days 6-13)

What happens: The brain releases FSH to stimulate follicles in the ovaries to mature an egg. Estrogen rises, rebuilding the lining.

  • Hormones: Rising Estrogen
  • Mood: Energized, social, confident, clear-headed
  • Common symptoms: Energy boost, glowing skin, rising libido
  • Self-care tip: Excellent time for higher-intensity workouts, social activities, and starting new projects! ☀️
Ovulation Phase (Day 14)

What happens: An LH surge triggers the release of the dominant egg from the ovary. The egg travels down the fallopian tube (fertile peak).

  • Hormones: Peak Estrogen & LH Surge
  • Mood: Peak confidence, social drive, high libido, feeling radiant
  • Common symptoms: Slippery cervical mucus, light twinges on one side of abdomen
  • Self-care tip: Nourish with fresh salads, channel creative energies, and enjoy active workouts. 🌸
Luteal Phase (Days 15-28)

What happens: The empty follicle turns into the corpus luteum, producing progesterone. Progesterone thickens the lining. If no fertilization occurs, hormones drop, causing shedding.

  • Hormones: High Progesterone, dropping Estrogen
  • Mood: Calm initially, transitioning to sensitivity, irritability, nesting
  • Common symptoms: Bloating, acne, cravings, breast tenderness, mood swings
  • Self-care tip: During the luteal phase, bloating and mood changes may happen due to hormonal shifts. Prioritize comforting herbal teas, warm baths, and warm stretching. tulip
Symptom Education

Period Symptoms Guide

Learn about common menstrual symptoms, why they happen, and gentle wellness relief methods.

Cramps (Dysmenorrhea)

Why it happens: Hormones called prostaglandins cause the uterus muscles to contract to shed its lining.

Wellness tips: Use a warm heating pad, drink warm chamomile/ginger tea, and practice gentle pelvis stretches.

Frequently Logged by You 🌸
Bloating

Why it happens: Progesterone fluctuations cause mild water and sodium retention, slowing digestion.

Wellness tips: Stay hydrated (signals body to release fluids), avoid salty snacks, and drink peppermint tea.

Frequently Logged by You 🌸
Hormonal Acne

Why it happens: Falling estrogen and rising progesterone trigger excess sebum production, clogging pores.

Wellness tips: Keep skin clean, apply soothing salicylic acid/tea tree oil, and limit processed sugar.

Frequently Logged by You 🌸
Fatigue

Why it happens: Energy levels drop because progesterone and estrogen levels fall rapidly before bleeding.

Wellness tips: Honor your body's request for rest, sleep 8+ hours, and drink nourishing hot broths.

Frequently Logged by You 🌸
Headaches & Migraines

Why it happens: Triggered by the sharp drop in estrogen levels just before bleeding begins.

Wellness tips: Sip electrolyte drinks, rest in a dark quiet room, and apply peppermint oil to temples.

Frequently Logged by You 🌸
Mood Swings

Why it happens: Hormonal shifts affect neurotransmitters like serotonin, which regulate happiness.

Wellness tips: Practice self-compassion, write down journal notes, and do gentle box breathing.

Frequently Logged by You 🌸
Food Cravings

Why it happens: Metabolic rate increases slightly during luteal phase; body craves quick glucose.

Wellness tips: Enjoy high-quality dark chocolate (magnesium-rich) or complex carbs like sweet potato.

Frequently Logged by You 🌸
Lower Back Pain

Why it happens: Prostaglandins and pelvic muscle spasms radiate pain into the lower back nerves.

Wellness tips: Try child's pose stretches, use lower back heat patches, and do warm Epsom salt baths.

Hormonal Conditions

Understanding PCOS

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common condition linked to hormone balance. It affects how the ovaries develop and release eggs, often caused by insulin resistance. 🌸

Common Indicators
  • Irregular periods or complete absence of menstrual cycles
  • Hormonal acne, excess facial/body hair (hirsutism)
  • Mild weight gain or difficulty regulating metabolism
  • Ovaries containing small follicles (fluid sacs)
Empowering Lifestyle Tips
  • Nutrition: Eat complex carbs, protein, and healthy fats to support insulin levels.
  • Movement: Focus on strength training and moderate walking over exhaustive cardio.
  • Sleep: Prioritize quality rest to regulate stress hormones.
Please Note:

This information is educational and not a medical diagnosis. Talk to a trusted doctor if you suspect PCOS.

Pain Awareness

Endometriosis Awareness

Endometriosis is a condition where tissue similar to the uterus lining grows outside of it (such as on ovaries or bowels). This tissue swells and sheds, causing severe localized inflammation and discomfort.

Understanding the Symptoms
  • Severe pelvic pain: Discomfort that is much stronger than normal period cramps and interferes with daily tasks.
  • Intercourse pain: Deep pain felt during or after sex.
  • Heavy flow: Excessive bleeding, spotting, or large clots.
  • Digestion pain: Pain when urinating or during bowel movements during your period.

Many women suffer for years before getting diagnosed. If your pain does not respond to painkillers or heating pads, seek professional help. Your pain is real and deserves treatment.

Advocate for Yourself

Keep a detailed log of your symptoms and pain score in PinkPetal. Print it or show it to your doctor during consultations to speed up your diagnosis.

Hygiene & Balance

Vaginal Health & Hygiene

Maintaining a healthy vaginal microbiome is essential for comfort and avoiding infections. Here's what you need to know about pH balance and hygiene.

Normal Discharge Guide
  • Clear & stretchy: Occurs during ovulation. Represents peak fertility.
  • White & thick: Normal at start/end of cycle, as long as it has no smell.
  • Watery & clear: Common after exercise or during high estrogen phases.
Warning: Thick white, clumpy (cottage-cheese-like) or gray-green discharge with strong odor may indicate a yeast or bacterial infection.
pH Scale & Vaginal Flora

A healthy vagina has an acidic pH (between 3.8 and 4.5). This acidity protects against bad bacteria.

Acidic (3.0) Normal (3.8-4.5) Alkaline (7.0+)
Safe Hygiene Practices

Use only warm water or soft, unfragranced wash for the external area (vulva). Avoid douching, as the vagina is self-cleaning and douching disrupts its natural pH balance.

Nutrition Guide

Nutrition During Your Period

Fueling your body with the right nutrients can dramatically ease bloating, fatigue, and cramping. Learn what foods to add and which ones to skip.

Nourishing Foods to Add
  • Iron-Rich: Spinach, beans, lentils, and red meat to replenish iron lost from bleeding.
  • Magnesium: Dark chocolate, almonds, and pumpkin seeds to relax pelvic muscles and ease cramps.
  • Hydration: Warm water, cucumber, and watermelon to reduce water retention and bloating.
Foods to Limit
  • Salty Snacks: Chips and processed foods increase water retention and worsen bloating.
  • Refined Sugar: Sweets and soda lead to sharp insulin spikes, worsening mood swings and energy drops.
  • Excess Caffeine: Coffee can constrict blood vessels, which may increase cramps.
Wellness Habits

Cycle-Based Self Care

Your body changes throughout the month. Aligning your exercise, skincare, and sleep routines with your cycle phases helps protect your energy. 🌸

  • Menstrual Phase Rest & Nourish

    Choose gentle walking, slow stretching, or restorative yin yoga. Focus on keeping warm, hydrating, and sleeping 8-9 hours.

  • Follicular Phase Build & Explore

    Your energy rises. Focus on strength training, aerobics, or high-intensity interval training (HIIT). Try new active skincare serums.

  • Ovulation Phase Shine & Connect

    Ovulation phase brings high energy levels 🌸. Take advantage of this with challenging exercises or group workouts. Keep your skincare light and simple.

  • Luteal Phase Slow Down & Protect

    Reduce high-intensity workouts and focus on pilates, moderate walking, or gentle swimming. Avoid harsh skincare products; focus on calming clay masks.

Fertility Science

Fertility & Ovulation Basics

Understanding ovulation is key to fertility management, whether you are trying to conceive or learning to track your cycle signals.

The Fertile Window

Sperm can survive in the body for up to 5 days, while an egg lives for 12-24 hours after release. This creates a 6-day fertile window: the 5 days before ovulation and ovulation day.

Cervical Mucus Signals

As estrogen levels rise, cervical discharge changes in texture. When it resembles raw egg whites (clear, slippery, and stretchy), it indicates peak fertility and helps sperm travel safely.

Basal Body Temperature (BBT)

Your resting body temperature rises slightly (about 0.5°F) immediately after ovulation occurs, caused by the surge in progesterone. Tracking BBT daily before getting out of bed helps confirm that ovulation has happened.

Health Safety

When to Consult a Doctor

While periods bring normal discomfort, severe pain or irregular bleeding is not something you have to live with. Listen to your body and consult a medical professional if you notice these warning signs.

Warning Signs checklist:
Extremely painful periods: Pain that is debilitating or doesn't improve with heating pads or common painkillers.
Excessive bleeding: Soaking through a pad or tampon in less than an hour for several hours in a row.
Missed periods: Not bleeding for 3 consecutive months when you are not pregnant or going through menopause.
Severe fatigue or dizziness: Feeling faint, short of breath, or constantly exhausted, which may indicate anemia.
Unusual discharge: Gray, green, or thick clumpy discharge with a strong odor or accompanied by itching/burning.
Persistent pelvic pain: Discomfort in the lower abdomen that continues throughout the month outside of your period.
